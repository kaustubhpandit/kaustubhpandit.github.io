Optical Coherence Tomography
OCT

Optical Coherence Tomography

OCT uses low-coherence interferometry to acquire micrometer-resolution cross-sectional images of biological tissue. My work investigates spectral-domain and swept-source OCT systems, focusing on signal processing pipelines for dispersion compensation, motion artifact correction, and speckle reduction.

I am particularly interested in OCT angiography (OCTA), which extracts blood flow contrast without exogenous agents by analyzing inter-frame decorrelation — enabling label-free vascular imaging at capillary resolution.

Pulse-Echo Ultrasound
Ultrasound

Pulse-Echo Ultrasound Imaging

Ultrasound imaging exploits the reflection of high-frequency acoustic waves at tissue interfaces. I work on plane-wave and diverging-wave compounding strategies that enable high frame-rate (>1000 fps) acquisition while maintaining image quality through coherent spatial compounding.

My interest spans beamforming algorithms (DAS, MV, DMAS), point spread function modeling, and advanced reconstruction techniques including compressed sensing and model-based iterative methods.

Modality 03
Super-Resolution Vascular Imaging
ULM

Ultrasound Localization Microscopy

ULM overcomes the acoustic diffraction limit by precisely localizing individual microbubble contrast agents across thousands of acquisitions and accumulating their positions into a super-resolved vascular map — analogous to STORM/PALM in fluorescence microscopy.

My work explores deep learning-based bubble detection and tracking, sparse recovery formulations, and fast acquisition strategies to reduce the prohibitively long acquisition times that limit current in-vivo ULM.

Photoacoustics

Photoacoustic Imaging

Photoacoustic imaging leverages the conversion of absorbed optical energy into broadband acoustic waves via the thermoelastic effect. This hybrid modality provides the optical contrast of light with the penetration depth of ultrasound, enabling label-free imaging of hemoglobin and other endogenous absorbers at centimeter depths.

I work on spectroscopic photoacoustics for blood oxygenation mapping (sO₂), model-based image reconstruction, and integrating PA with co-registered ultrasound for multi-modal tissue characterization.

Machine Learning for Imaging
ML / AI

Deep Learning for Biomedical Imaging

Across all imaging modalities, I apply deep learning to solve challenging inverse problems: image reconstruction, denoising, super-resolution, segmentation, and quantification. Neural networks trained on physics-informed simulations can dramatically reduce acquisition requirements and improve image quality.

Key architectures I work with include U-Nets for segmentation, unrolled iterative networks for reconstruction, and physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) for wave propagation modeling.
