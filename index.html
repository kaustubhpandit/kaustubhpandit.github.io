About

Imaging the invisible,
one photon at a time.

I'm Kaustubh (Krish) — a senior in Mechanical Engineering (Honors) at the University of Oklahoma, working in the Biophotonic Imaging Laboratory under Profs. Qinggong Tang and Tri Vu. My research integrates computational modeling, imaging platforms, and wet-lab validation to develop noninvasive diagnostic methods.

I specialize in Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) and Photoacoustic Imaging (PAI) for human organ evaluation — including liver viability, kidney perfusion, and cardiac tissue characterization. My work with Ultrasound Localization Microscopy (ULM) enables super-resolved vascular imaging of transplant organs.

With 7 peer-reviewed publications, 4 patent filings, and oral presentations at SPIE Photonics West and BMES, I'm seeking a PhD to continue advancing translational biomedical imaging.

Featured Research

Imaging Modalities

Four complementary windows into biological structure and function.

OCT B-scan simulation
OCT

Optical Coherence Tomography

Micron-scale cross-sectional imaging using low-coherence interferometry. High-speed A-scan acquisition enables real-time volumetric imaging of biological tissue with ~5–10 μm axial resolution.

Learn more
Ultrasound B-mode simulation
Ultrasound

Pulse-Echo Ultrasound Imaging

Acoustic wave propagation and echo-based reconstruction for deep tissue imaging. Beamforming, compounding, and plane-wave imaging techniques for high frame-rate acquisition.

Learn more
ULM microbubble tracking
ULM

Ultrasound Localization Microscopy

Super-resolution vascular imaging by localizing individual microbubble contrast agents. Breaks the diffraction limit to reveal sub-wavelength vascular architecture and blood flow dynamics.

Learn more
Photoacoustic wave simulation
Photoacoustics

Photoacoustic Imaging

Pulsed laser excitation generates broadband acoustic waves via the thermoelastic effect. Enables label-free imaging of optical absorbers (hemoglobin, melanin) with ultrasonic resolution at depth.
